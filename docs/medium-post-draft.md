# I Built a System That Runs My Social Media. Here's Why I'm Giving It Away.

It was 11pm on a Tuesday.

I had three tabs open: a half-written LinkedIn post I'd been staring at for forty minutes, a folder of photos I'd been meaning to caption since last month, and a voice note from myself — recorded at 7am when the idea was good — that I still hadn't listened back to.

I had ideas. I always have ideas. What I didn't have was a system to do anything with them.

I closed the laptop. The content didn't get made. The idea in the voice note expired. And I showed up the next week doing exactly the same thing — except now I also felt behind.

I'm an AI practitioner. I build automation systems for a living. And I was still doing this manually.

That gap bothered me enough to do something about it.

---

## The problem isn't ideas. It's the distance between the idea and the post.

Most advice about content creation focuses on the wrong thing. It tells you to batch your content, to use a calendar, to be consistent. All of this is true and none of it addresses the actual friction.

The friction isn't discipline. It's distance.

The distance between "I have something to say" and "it is posted, captioned, formatted, scheduled, and live on the platform" is enormous. And when you're a solo operator — or a small team — that distance costs you hours you don't have.

I was losing somewhere between 5 and 8 hours a week to content production. Not creating — producing. The captioning, the resizing, the scheduling, the second-guessing. The machine work.

I decided to automate it.

---

## What I built

I call it FLOWSTATE. It's an AI-powered automation system that takes content from "dropped a file in a folder" to "live on your platforms" with one approval step in between.

Here's how it works in three steps:

**1. Drop content.** You move a photo, video, or rough idea into a folder. That's the whole input.

**2. AI does the production.** The system analyses what you dropped, writes 3 caption options in your voice tuned for each platform, and sends you a preview on WhatsApp. You reply Y1, Y2, or Y3 to pick one. It takes about 10 seconds.

**3. It posts.** Content goes live automatically. You get a weekly digest of comments. You never have to open the apps.

The thing I care most about: your voice stays yours. The AI doesn't write generic captions. It writes in a voice brief you define — your vocabulary, your tone, your way of ending a sentence. The output sounds like you on a good day, not like a social media manager who's never met you.

---

## What this unlocked for me

I don't check Instagram anymore. Not because I'm not posting — I'm posting more consistently than I ever have — but because I don't need to. The machine handles it.

I spend about 10 minutes a week on social media. That's it. The rest goes back to the work I actually want to do.

More than the time savings, though: my content got better. When I'm not under pressure to produce something every day, I actually have space to have ideas worth sharing. The quality went up when the production pressure went down.

---

## Why I'm open-sourcing it

I could have turned this into a SaaS. That thought crossed my mind.

But I kept coming back to something: the women I want this to help are not the ones who can afford another $50/month subscription. They're the ones who are already doing everything — building a business, raising a family, creating content, staying visible — and have run out of hours.

The best thing I can do is give them the system and let them run it themselves.

FLOWSTATE is open source. The repo is on GitHub at `github.com/sanyuja/flowstate`. It includes:

- The prompt templates that power the AI caption system (adapt them to any niche or voice)
- Two worked examples showing input → output
- A reference n8n workflow you can import and modify
- A full setup guide — you can have a working system in an afternoon

You don't need a developer. You need an afternoon and a willingness to set up a few free accounts.
