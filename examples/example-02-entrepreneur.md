# Example 02: Entrepreneur / Founder Use Case

**Prompt used:** [02 — Mental Load to Plan](../prompts/02-mental-load-to-plan.md)

---

## Input

> "I have a product launch next Thursday, two client calls tomorrow, an invoice I haven't sent yet, my website copy needs updating, I've been putting off recording a welcome video for two weeks, and I feel like I'm behind on everything. Also need to reply to 3 DMs about working together."

---

## Output

### 1. Today's Top 3

1. **Send the invoice** — this is money sitting on the table. Do it first, before anything else, in the next 30 minutes.
2. **Prep for tomorrow's client calls** — spend 20 minutes per call reviewing notes, so you show up ready and confident.
3. **Reply to the 3 DMs** — these are warm leads. A 2-minute reply now could be $X in the door next week.

Everything else is secondary to these three.

---

### 2. Full Prioritized Task List

**Must Do Today**
- [ ] Send outstanding invoice (30 min)
- [ ] Prep for client call #1 (20 min)
- [ ] Prep for client call #2 (20 min)
- [ ] Reply to 3 DMs about working together (15 min)

**Should Do This Week (Before Thursday)**
- [ ] Finalize launch plan for Thursday — what goes out, when, to whom (2 hours)
- [ ] Update website copy — focus only on the page most people land on first (2 hours)

**Can Wait Until After Launch**
- [ ] Record welcome video — this is not urgent and creative work needs mental space

**Consider Dropping / Delegating**
- [ ] Website copy update — can you hire someone for this? Or do the minimum viable version?
- [ ] Welcome video — is this actually blocking revenue right now? If not, schedule it for after the launch.

---

### 3. Suggested Daily Schedule

| Time | Block |
|------|-------|
| 9:00–9:30am | Send invoice + reply to DMs (money tasks first) |
| 9:30–10:00am | Prep for client call #1 |
| 10:00–11:00am | Client call #1 |
| 11:00–11:30am | Break + prep for call #2 |
| 11:30am–12:30pm | Client call #2 |
| 12:30–1:30pm | Lunch — actual lunch, not at your desk |
| 1:30–3:30pm | Launch plan: what goes out Thursday, to whom, on what channels |
| 3:30–4:00pm | Buffer / catch-up / email |
| 4:00pm | Stop. Tomorrow is a big day. |

---

### 4. What to Delegate or Automate

- **Invoice** — use an invoicing tool (Wave, HoneyBook, Dubsado) so future invoices send automatically on project completion
- **DM replies** — create 2–3 saved reply templates so this takes 2 minutes, not 20
- **Website copy** — if budget allows, hire a copywriter for one session; if not, use an AI prompt to draft it

---

### 5. One Thing to Let Go Of Today

The welcome video. It has been on your list for two weeks because part of you knows it's not the priority right now. You have a launch in 6 days. Give yourself permission to record it the week after. Schedule it now so it's not floating.

---

*Input was one overwhelmed paragraph. Output is a clear day with a finish line.*
