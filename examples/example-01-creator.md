# Example 01: Content Creator Use Case

**Prompt used:** [01 — Idea to Content Week](../prompts/01-idea-to-content-week.md)

---

## Input

> "I want to talk about how I stopped trying to post every day and my engagement actually went up."

---

## Output

### 1. Instagram Reel Script (60 seconds)

**Hook (0–3 sec):**
"I used to post every single day. My engagement tanked. Here's what I did instead."

**Main point (3–50 sec):**
"Last year I was showing up daily — stories, reels, carousels — and burning out completely. My numbers were going nowhere. So I stopped. I cut back to 3 times a week and actually put thought into what I posted.

Within 30 days, my saves doubled. My DMs tripled. People started saying 'I feel like I actually know you.'

The algorithm doesn't reward frequency. It rewards *resonance*. And you cannot resonate when you're running on empty."

**CTA (50–60 sec):**
"If you've been grinding and wondering why it's not working — try this. Post less. Say more. Drop a 🌊 if you needed to hear this."

---

### 2. LinkedIn Post

Posting every day nearly broke me.

For six months I was consistent — content every single day. Reels, posts, stories. I was doing everything right.

My engagement was flat. My DMs were quiet. I was exhausted.

So I stopped. I cut to 3 posts a week and focused on quality over output.

30 days later: saves up 2x, followers actually messaging me, and I had energy left at the end of the day.

The insight: people don't follow accounts. They follow *people*. And you can't be a person when you're a content machine.

What's one thing you've stopped doing that actually made things better?

---

### 3. Twitter/X Thread

**Tweet 1:** I posted every day for 6 months. My reach tanked. Then I stopped — and everything changed. Here's what I learned: 🧵

**Tweet 2:** Consistency ≠ frequency. I confused the two for way too long. Showing up every day doesn't mean showing up with something worth saying.

**Tweet 3:** When I cut to 3x/week, I had time to actually *think*. My posts got more specific. More personal. More real. The algorithm noticed.

**Tweet 4:** The metric that changed most wasn't likes. It was saves. People were bookmarking things to come back to. That's the signal that actually matters.

**Tweet 5:** Post less. Say more. Protect your energy. The internet will still be here tomorrow. 🌊

---

5. You don't need to post every day. Here's the data.

### 5. Podcast Talking Points

- The "consistency" trap: why we confuse showing up with showing up with something worth saying
- The moment I decided to stop (what it felt like, what I was afraid of)
- What I changed: from daily → 3x week, and what I cut first
- The metrics that actually shifted (saves, DMs, follower quality)
- What I'd tell someone who's burning out from content right now
