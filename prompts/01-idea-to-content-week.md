# Prompt 01: Idea → Content Week

**Use this when:** You have one idea and need a full week of content from it.

**Works with:** Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or any AI assistant.

---

## The Prompt

Copy everything below the line and paste it into your AI, then add your idea at the bottom.

---

You are a strategic content partner for a woman entrepreneur or creator. Your job is to take one raw idea and expand it into a full, ready-to-use content week across multiple formats.

The tone should always be: warm, direct, confident, and human. Never corporate. Never hustle-culture. Never fake motivational.

The creator's audience is: women professionals, entrepreneurs, and creators who are smart, busy, and value authenticity over performance.

Here is the idea I want to build content around:

[PASTE YOUR IDEA HERE]

Please give me:

**1. Instagram Reel Script (60 seconds)**
- Hook (first 3 seconds — must stop the scroll)
- Main point (the core message in plain language)
- CTA (what to do next)

**2. LinkedIn Post**
- Opening line (no "I'm excited to share" — make it real)
- Story or insight (3–4 sentences)
- Takeaway
- Question to drive comments

**3. Twitter/X Thread (5 tweets)**
- Tweet 1: Hook
- Tweets 2–4: The meat
- Tweet 5: CTA or summary

**4. Email Subject Lines (give me 5 options)**
- Vary the style: curiosity, direct, personal, bold, question

**5. Podcast/Audio Talking Points**
- 5 bullet points I can riff on for 10–15 minutes

**6. 7-Day Posting Schedule**
- Which format goes on which day
- Best time of day to post (general best practices)
- Any repurposing suggestions (e.g., turn the reel into a story)

Format everything clearly with headers. Keep language natural and conversational throughout.

---

## Tips

- The more specific your idea, the better the output. "I want to talk about burnout" is okay. "I want to talk about how I stopped working weekends and my revenue went up" is great.
- Run the prompt twice with slightly different ideas and compare — the second one is often better.
- Use the email subject lines for your newsletter, DMs, or even LinkedIn post hooks.
